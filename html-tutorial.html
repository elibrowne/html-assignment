Avocado Information

Avocados come from the avocado tree and are technically berries. They have one large pit. Avocados are both delicious and nutritious.

History

Avocados originated in south-central Mexico. The word avocado comes from aguacate in Spanish, which is based off of Nahuatl's āhuacatl.

Nutrition

Avocados have many nutrients and vitamins, and they are generally a very healthy food. These benefits include B vitamins, vitamin C, vitamin E, and potassium - in fact, avocados contain more potassium than bananas. Avocados are known for their healthy fats and fiber.

Nutrient Amount in per 100g avocado
Fiber 6.7 g
Monounsaturated Fat 9.8 g
Protein 2 g
Potassium 485 mg

Recipes

Avocados are a perfect addition to many recipes! Some great recipes include:

  • Salads
  • Smoothies
  • Guacamole
  • Tacos
  • Avocado Toast
Here is one recipe for guacamole from AllRecipes.
  1. Mash 2 avocados in a bowl.
  2. Stir in 1 chopped onion and one minced clove of garlic.
  3. Add in a chopped tomato.
  4. Juice a lime and add the lime juice to the mixture.
  5. Add salt and pepper as you'd like!
