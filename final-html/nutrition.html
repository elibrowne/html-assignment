nutrients that pack a punch • • • •


vitamin c


Vitamin C is a super important nutrient. It works as an antioxidant and even heals wounds! A cup of avocado has a quarter of the daily recommended intake.

Learn more

potassium


Bananas are known for their potassium, but avocados have even more! Potassium helps send nerve signals and regulate muscle contractions. A cup of avocado has 485mg.

Learn more

vitamin b5


A cup of avocado has nearly 30% of the daily recommended vitamin B5. It's a super important vitamin that helps with healthy eyes, the nervous system, and in making red blood cells.

Learn more

folate


One of the B vitamins, folate is super important. It's needed to make DNA, to split cells, and to convert carbs into energy! A cup of avocado has a fifth of the daily recommended folate intake.

Learn more

• • • • what the studies say


avocados against osteoporosis

Osteoporosis is a condition in which bones become brittle and easily broken. Avocados contain vitamin K, which has been shown to regulate osteoclast production. This nutrient is super important in keeping bones strong! Vitamin C and Folate have also been shown to have positive impacts on bone health.


Learn more about osteoporosis here.

avocados working to fight cancer

Although they obviously aren't a miracle cancer cure, avocados have been shown to inhibit the growth of certain cancer cells while encouraging lymphocytes, which are immune system cells. Some have suggested that avocados could be used for lowering the side effects of chemotherapy in cancer patients.


Read a summary here.

fuel your gut bacteria

Gut bacteria isn't a bad thing: it's super important to our digestive systems! Fiber in avocados promotes the growth of positive gut bacteria, which has a variety of health benefits for digestive health and even in preventing irritable bowel syndrome and Crohn's disease.


Learn more about fiber here.

healthy heart, happy heart

Did you know that fat can be healthy? Monounsaturated fatty acids, which are very prevalent in avocados, are super helpful. Oleic acid, the most common type of fat in avocados, may reduce the risk of coronary heart disease, one of the leading causes of death in the United States.


Learn more about oleic acid here.
