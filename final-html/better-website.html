the facts on avocados • • • •


get familiar


Avocados are berries from the avocado tree with one large pit. Today, avocados are popular not only for their taste but also their nutritous value.

know your roots


Avocados originated in south-central Mexico -- "avocado" comes from aguacate in Spanish, which comes from Nahuatl's āhuacatl.

power up


Avocados are very nutritious: you'll find B vitamins, vitamin C, vitamin E, and potassium in the fruit. Avocados are well known for their healthy fats and fiber.

stats don't lie


In every hundred grams of avocado, you'll find 6.7 grams of fiber, 9.8 grams of monounsaturated fat (the good kind!), and 2 grams of protein. Eat up!


• • • • what you can do with them


easy guacamole

You'll need two avocados, an onion, a clove of garlic, a tomato, a lime, and some salt and pepper.

  1. Mash the avocados in a bowl.
  2. Stir in a chopped onion and a minced clove of garlic.
  3. Add in a chopped tomato.
  4. Juice a lime and add the lime juice to the mixture.
  5. Add salt and pepper as you'd like!

meyer lemon avocado toast

You'll need two slices of toast and an avocado. You can also add some cilantro, lemon juice, lemon zest, salt, cayenne pepper, and chia seeds.

  1. Toast the bread to your desired color.
  2. Mash the avocado in a bowl with the other ingredients.
  3. Spread across toast! You're done.
